The Problem

A house move is a boiler-cover decision waiting to happen.

When someone moves home they take on a boiler they didn't choose, can't see the service history for, and have no warranty on. It's the moment anxiety about breakdown is highest and loyalty to any provider is zero. Yet cover providers mostly address these customers late, on a price-comparison page, competing on price vs the rest of the market.

Commercial Plays

Five ways move data pays for itself.

Move signals aren't just a lead list, they touch acquisition cost, retention, loss ratio and the heat-pump pipeline at the same time.

🔒

1 · Defend retention ★ biggest prize

With a 500k base, c.30k customers move home each year. Overlay the feed to catch them and trigger a "take your cover to your new home" flow, stopping policies lapsing silently when the property changes.

🎯

2 · Acquire at move-in

Reach new homeowners in the 21–35 day window with a "new home, unknown boiler, get covered + a first service" offer, before they hit a comparison site. Direct, higher-loyalty than price aggregators.

📊

3 · Price on real risk

EPC + heating-system + boiler-age enrichment lets underwriting avoid adverse selection, pricing or screening the oldest, highest-claim boilers instead of flat-rating every mover.

♨️

4 · Feed the heat-pump pipeline

Movers in inefficient (D–G EPC) homes with ageing gas boilers are the prime renovation cohort. Flag them for heat-pump and A-rated replacement offers in the post-move window.

🏘️

5 · Win new landlords

Buy-to-let and tenure signals identify newly-purchased rental properties needing annual gas safety (CP12) and landlord cover, a distinct, recurring-revenue segment.
